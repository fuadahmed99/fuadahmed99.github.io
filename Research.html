Publication

Title: Effects of Minimum Quantity Lubrication (MQL) on Surface Roughness in Milling Al Alloy 383 / ADC 12 Using Nano Hybrid Cutting Fluid

Authors: MSH Ador, S Kabir, Fuad Ahmed, F Ahmad, S Adil
Publisher : Transdisciplinary Research and Education Center for Green Technologies, Kyushu University
DOI : doi.org/10.5109/6625790

The study yielded the following conclusions:
• Al2O3 /CNT nano-particle mixed with EVO oil , make the nanofluid increase heat capacity, thermal conductivity, dynamic viscosity.
• The surface finish was significantly enhanced, owing to the large reduction in temperature at the tool face caused by the use of Nanofluid. Reduction in temperature is obtained due to better lubrication and higher thermal conductivity of the nanofluid which helps in reducing roughness.
• Compared to dry and conventional, Nanofluids significantly reduced the surface roughness.
• The ANN models outperformed the RSM models when it came to predicting surface roughness accuracy in the range where they were trained. The ANN models had a stronger correlation and a lower error rate than the RSM model.


Book Chapter: Review of new developments in different types of sensors over the past 15 years

Authors: MSH Ador, P Bhattacharjee, S Kabir, MT Ahmed, Fuad Ahmed, IA Choudhury
Publisher: Reference Module in Materials Science and Materials Engineering, Elsevier

DOI : https://doi.org/10.1016/B978-0-323-96020-5.00043-1

Key Objective:
• To outline different types of available sensors in recent years.
• To review recent developments and fabrications of sensors.
• To take a look at the most recent innovations in the world of sensing materials.
• To discuss about the state of research in the last 15 years in the area of sensor applications.
• To analyze the future trend is to make sensor devices more affordable, smaller, and energy-efficient.
• To Identify the major challenges in the development of sensors technologies and limitations
• To set a research outline for the future that comprehends the evaluation of industry 4.0 on sensor technologies selection of modern sensor materials.
